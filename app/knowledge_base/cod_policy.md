# Cash-on-Delivery (COD) Policy (Demo)

Demonstration COD policy used to ground COD-dispute triage. Not a binding policy.

## COD basics

- COD is available on most orders up to **₹50,000**; some festival and prepaid-only
  deals exclude COD.
- The amount collected must **exactly match** the order total shown in the app at the
  time of order placement, including delivery fees and any handling charge.
- Delivery partners must provide a digital or printed invoice on request.

## Overcharge disputes

- If the delivery agent collects **more than the app-displayed total**, the customer is
  entitled to a refund of the difference.
- Required evidence: order ID, app screenshot of the total, and the amount paid (UPI
  reference or cash receipt).
- Confirmed overcharges are refunded to wallet/bank in **7–10 business days**.

## Failed / partial COD

- If the customer pays only part of the amount, the order may be held; escalate to
  logistics rather than resolving automatically.
- Repeated COD refusals can lead to COD being disabled for an account (informational
  only; triage does not enforce this).

## Fraud / safety

- Customers should never pay extra "delivery tips", "unlock fees", or "GST top-ups" at
  the door. Such requests are policy violations and should be **escalated**.
- Never share OTPs before paying; the COD OTP confirms delivery, not payment.

## Triage stance

- COD disputes are **medium-to-high severity**. Verify the displayed total versus amount
  paid, cite this policy, and mark the case as requiring confirmation before any refund.
